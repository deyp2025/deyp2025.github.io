About

I am an Assistant Professor of Mathematics at Millsaps College. My research is in functional analysis and operator theory, with a focus on n-potent operators, projections and isometries on Banach spaces, and spectral decompositions, where I study how the geometric structure of the underlying space influences operator behavior.

I am enthusiastic about undergraduate teaching and mentoring at both introductory and upper-level courses. I enjoy helping students develop mathematical intuition alongside rigor, and guiding them toward meaningful research experiences.

Research

My research lies in functional analysis and operator theory, focusing on operators on Banach spaces. I am particularly interested in projections and isometries, and in understanding their structural and spectral properties. My current work explores how geometric features of Banach spaces influence the existence and behavior of spectral and Riesz projections, as well as related operator classes. I also study problems that arise at the interface of operator theory and other areas of analysis.
